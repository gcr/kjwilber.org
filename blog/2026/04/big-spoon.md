## Big spoon as yogurt enabler

Throughout the years, I've tried and failed to purchase yogurt in bulk. Like everybody, I enjoy consuming yogurt, but I would always get those 5.3pz chobani cups because they’re cheap and plentiful, a resource that’s easy to procure on sight among a variety of operational theatres.

But even though they’re cheap in isolation, the cost starts to add up if you get ambushed by many at once. As with all grocery shopping dilemmas the solution of course is to shop in bulk. Get one of the big ones and “eat the elephant one bite at a time.” In my hubris, I often did exactly the first half of that. “Sure! I can eat a healthy breakfast that requires modest preparation healthy morning. Maybe it could be like a lifestyle change,” I would think to myself **stewing in my arrogance**. I’d then leave the large container to sit unopened in my fridge for weeks and throw it away shamefully.

Part of this is ADHD attention blindness, but part of this is definitely the opportunity cost of ruining a perfectly good clean bowl by ladling a slop of yogurt into it. No thanks! Who has time to wash one extra dish later when I could just use disposable plastic instead?

The issue disappears when you have a Big Spoon: just eat from the large container! Take the yogurt out, feast ravenously, then put the surplus back.

With practice, you can develop a more advanced tactic: grab some blueberries or jam and drop them *straight into the container*.

I tried this for the first time this morning, so I’m sure it will stick. Maybe it could be like a lifestyle change.
